Pythagorean Theorem Proof

Consider a large square with side length \(a + b\) and a smaller square with side length \(c\) inside it, tangent to the sides of the larger square. The area of the large square can be expressed in two ways:

\[ (a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2 \]

The area of the large square is also equal to the sum of the areas of the four right triangles with legs \(a\) and \(b\) and the area of the smaller square with side length \(c\):

\[ (a + b)^2 = 4 \left( \frac{1}{2}ab \right) + c^2 \]

Simplifying the right-hand side:

\[ (a + b)^2 = 2ab + c^2 \]

Setting the two expressions for the area of the large square equal gives:

\[ a^2 + 2ab + b^2 = 2ab + c^2 \]

Subtracting \(2ab\) from both sides results in the Pythagorean theorem:

\[ a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \]
